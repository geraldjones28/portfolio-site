Keep Moving Forward!

2026-02-13

Big moves happening this week. I applied for a work program to try to get some real-world experience in the IT field, and I also applied for a direct internship through CodePath that'll be happening in June. Both are exciting prospects, but I'd be lying if I said I wasn't nervous. Putting yourself out there is always a little scary, but that's where the growth happens.

Outside of that, I'm working hard to finish my degree and complete the CompTIA trifecta: A+, Network+, and Security+. Core 1 is done, and I'm deep into A+ Core 2 studies right now. Honestly, a lot of it is Windows stuff I already know, but it's good to formalize that knowledge and fill in the gaps. Onwards we go.

On the project side, I'm working on a simple administrator login UI built with JavaFX as part of a team. The project itself isn't anything crazy, but the real value is in the team collaboration and learning how to work together on a shared codebase. Those soft skills matter just as much as the technical ones.

Speaking of workflow, I've been learning a ton through implementing Claude Code into my development process. The productivity jump is real. I can focus on the architecture and logic while it handles the boilerplate. It's like pair programming with someone who never gets tired.

Another thing that's been a game-changer: the Pomodoro method. Without it, I get about 2 hours of deep work done. With it, I'm hitting 5 to 7 hours consistently. That's not a marginal improvement, that's a 3x multiplier. If you're not using it, try it. 25 minutes on, 5 minutes off. Feels good, man.

On a lighter note, I picked up a neat little synthesizer, a Korg FM2, that I like to mess around with whenever I get any increments of free time. There's something about making weird sounds that resets the brain between study sessions.

Keep moving forward, as Meet the Robinsons (favorite animated movie) says. :)
